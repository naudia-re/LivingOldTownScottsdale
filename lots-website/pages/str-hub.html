The Rules

Scottsdale STR rules, in plain english

The headlines you should know before you start touring. Not legal advice — and the regulations evolve. Always confirm with your agent and the City of Scottsdale before you offer.

01

You Need a Permit

Every STR operating in Scottsdale must register with the City and carry an active permit. There's an annual fee, an inspection requirement, and a permit number that must appear on every listing.

02

Liability Insurance Is Required

Scottsdale mandates a minimum liability policy specifically covering short-term rental use. Generic homeowner policies don't qualify — you need an STR endorsement or a dedicated STR policy.

03

HOAs Can Still Say No

A City permit doesn't override your HOA. Most Old Town condos are NOT STR-eligible — only a specific subset of buildings have CC&Rs that allow rentals under 30 days. We track the approved list for you.

04

Noise & Occupancy Caps Are Enforced

Scottsdale has real teeth here: documented complaints, occupancy limits tied to bedroom count, and quiet hours that are actively monitored. One bad guest can cost you a permit suspension.

The Agent

Why the right Scottsdale STR agent matters more than the building

Short-term rental real estate is its own discipline inside Scottsdale real estate. Pro formas, comparable Airbnb data, lender quirks, insurance carriers, HOA risk — the agent you pick decides whether the math actually pencils.

Most agents in Phoenix can show you an Old Town Scottsdale condo. Very few can underwrite one as a short-term rental investment. The difference between the two is everything.

An experienced Scottsdale STR agent doesn't quote you a generic ROI. They pull actual comparable Airbnb data for the specific building, model nightly rates by season, plug in real cleaning and management fees, layer in Scottsdale's permit cost and STR tax registration, and hand you a pro forma you can underwrite from. That work is the difference between a property that clears 70% occupancy and one that drags at 45%.

They also know the things that aren't on the MLS. Which HOAs are quietly drafting CC&R amendments that will kill STR use within the next 24 months. Which lenders actually write loans on STR-zoned properties (most won't). Which insurance carriers carry a real Scottsdale STR endorsement. Which property managers, cleaners, photographers, and supply vendors are worth bringing in — and which to avoid.

The Selling Sonoran Group has closed STR-eligible properties in nearly every Old Town building that allows them. We've underwritten hundreds of pro formas, walked clients through the Scottsdale permit process, and built a vendor network that hands new investors a turnkey operation on day one. That's the work. The listing is the easy part.

Six questions to ask any Scottsdale STR agent before you sign

  • How many STR-eligible properties have you closed in the last 12 months in Scottsdale?
  • Can you show me a pro forma you built for another client, with the actual numbers you used?
  • Which Old Town condo buildings do you consider STR-safe long-term, and which are on the watch list?
  • Do you have a lender who writes STR-zoned property loans without hammering my rate?
  • Who is your go-to STR insurance carrier and property manager — and are they available right now?
  • Have you helped an out-of-state buyer close on a Scottsdale STR remotely?

FAQ

Old Town Scottsdale STR investing, answered

The questions we get every week from first-time STR buyers, out-of-state investors, and portfolio scalers. The honest answers, not the brochure version.

Can you legally Airbnb a property in Old Town Scottsdale?

Yes — with the right setup. Short-term rentals are legal in Scottsdale, but every operator must register with the City, carry an active permit, list the permit number on every listing, and hold STR-specific liability insurance. Beyond the City layer, your HOA's CC&Rs control whether your specific building allows rentals under 30 days. Most Old Town condos do not allow it — only a specific subset (Optima Kierland, The Mark, Vue Scottsdale, 7180 Optima, Envy, and a few others) have STR-friendly CC&Rs. We confirm CC&Rs and pull HOA meeting minutes for any unit we underwrite.

Which Old Town Scottsdale condo buildings allow short-term rentals?

The current short list of STR-friendly Old Town condo communities includes Optima Kierland, The Mark, Vue Scottsdale, 7180 Optima, Envy, and Inspiration Pointe. A handful of other buildings allow 30+ day rentals only, which is a different financial model. The list shifts — HOAs occasionally amend CC&Rs to restrict or unlock STR use. We keep the live list updated and flag buildings at risk of policy change. See the current list above (account required).

What's a realistic ROI on an Old Town Scottsdale short-term rental?

Underwritten correctly, well-positioned STR-approved units in Old Town generally cash-flow with 7–10% ROI on cash invested, with top performers clearing 11–13%. The variance is huge though — same building, two units, ROI can swing 4 points based on floor, view, layout, furnishing quality, and pricing strategy. Anyone quoting you a flat "Old Town STRs return X%" without seeing the unit isn't doing the work. We run a unit-specific pro forma on every property before we recommend you offer.

Do I need a Scottsdale STR permit before closing?

No — the City of Scottsdale STR permit is filed after you take title, since the permit attaches to the property and owner. But you should not close without confirming that the building allows STR use and the unit can be permitted. We pull HOA documents and confirm Scottsdale zoning compatibility before you waive contingencies. Once you close, the permit application takes ~30 days, requires an inspection, and renews annually.

I'm buying from out of state — can I do this remotely?

Constantly. Most of our STR buyers are out of state. We handle FaceTime walkthroughs, detailed video tours, drone footage for the lot, and condition-specific recordings before you board a plane. We coordinate inspections, sign-and-deliver disclosures via DocuSign, and connect you with vetted property managers so the unit is operating before you even see it in person. We've closed Scottsdale STRs for buyers in California, Washington, New York, Texas, and Canada — the playbook is the playbook.

Can my existing real estate agent help me with an STR, or do I need a specialist?

Honest answer: most agents can help you buy a Scottsdale property, but only an STR specialist can help you buy one that operates. The pro forma, the HOA risk read, the lender connections, the insurance carriers, the post-close vendor network — that's a specialty discipline. Buying an STR with a generic agent isn't dangerous in the transaction, but it routinely costs investors 1–3 ROI points over the first three years of ownership. That's tens of thousands of dollars on a $1M unit.

What's the difference between investing in an Old Town condo vs. a single-family home for STR?

Condos in STR-approved Old Town buildings are simpler operationally (amenities included, HOA handles exterior, walkable to the entertainment district) but capped on guest count and lower on ADR ceiling. Single-family homes outside an HOA — especially in 85250, 85251, or 85257 with a pool and 3+ bedrooms — can clear materially higher ADRs ($500–$800+) and accommodate group bookings, but require more active management and carry higher operating expenses (pool service, landscaping, larger turnovers). The right answer depends on your goals, capital, and management appetite.
