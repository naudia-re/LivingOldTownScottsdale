The Rules

Scottsdale STR rules, in plain english

The headlines you should know before you start touring. Not legal advice — and the regulations evolve. Always confirm with your agent and the City of Scottsdale before you offer.

01

You Need a Permit

Every STR operating in Scottsdale must register with the City and carry an active permit. There's an annual fee, an inspection requirement, and a permit number that must appear on every listing.

02

Liability Insurance Is Required

Scottsdale mandates a minimum liability policy specifically covering short-term rental use. Generic homeowner policies don't qualify — you need an STR endorsement or a dedicated STR policy.

03

HOAs Can Still Say No

A City permit doesn't override your HOA. Most Old Town condos are NOT STR-eligible — only a specific subset of buildings have CC&Rs that allow rentals under 30 days. We track the approved list for you.

04

Noise & Occupancy Caps Are Enforced

Scottsdale has real teeth here: documented complaints, occupancy limits tied to bedroom count, and quiet hours that are actively monitored. One bad guest can cost you a permit suspension.
